In October 2025, a member of our team - Paweł Andrzejczuk – arborist expert and dendrologist from Wrocław - after a one-year specialized course at the Arboriculture Academy passed with very good results a difficult, demanding industry examination, obtaining the prestigious ETT - European Tree Technician certification title. The certification is awarded by the European Arboriculture Council (EAC - European Arboriculture Council). The ETT industry credentials certify the highest qualifications at the pan-European level in the field of knowledge and competences in arboriculture, tree care.

Paweł Andrzejczuk's many years of practical experience in dendrology and arboriculture gained in the active preparation of all specialized studies concerning trees – **tree inventories, dendrology management plans, opinions and dendrology expert assessments**, as well as the provision of services such as **dendrology supervision, tree inspections for safety, consulting on tree safety** (or **safety in the vicinity of trees**) combined with the newly acquired industry credentials (ETT certificate – European Tree Technician) guarantees the quality of services of our company Pracownia IKROPKA at the highest, European level.

**Scope of ETT European Tree Technician credentials – authorized arborist and dendrology specialist**

- specialist in organizing, conducting works / managing works on trees and in their vicinity
- specialist in supervising works on trees and in their vicinity, verifying the justification and correctness of actions performed on trees (work acceptances)
- specialist in organizing, managing and supervising the work of ETW (European Tree Worker) and other persons working on trees
- specialist in analyzing documentation concerning trees, legal issues, quantity surveying and cost estimating of works.

**On the organization examining and awarding the ETT – European Tree Technician certification:**

*European Arboricultural Council (EAC) – European Arboriculture Council is a European organization aimed at raising the status and professional level of competence in tree cultivation, which is achieved through research and education. The EAC includes arboriculture organizations from Austria, Belgium, Croatia, Czech Republic, Denmark, Finland, Germany, France, Italy, Netherlands, Norway, Poland, Romania, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland and the United Kingdom. The EAC achieves its goals by organizing uniform training:*

- *European Tree Worker, whose task is to educate highly specialized workers directly performing works related to the care and cultivation of trees,*
- *European Tree Technician, whose task is to educate highly specialized inspectors supervising tree care works.*

*The result of these trainings are certificates recognized in the countries of the European Union, as well as, under an agreement with the International Society of Arboriculture (ISA), outside the European Union – worldwide. (source: Arboriculture Academy)*
