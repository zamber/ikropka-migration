A dendrology management plan is prepared for the management of existing greenery or for the needs of an investment requiring maintenance works, removals or transplanting. We invite you to cooperate!


## What is a dendrology management plan?

A dendrology management plan, i.e. a dendrology expert assessment with a tree stand management design, is prepared on the basis of a current, detailed tree inventory. The plan is drawn up in order to issue recommendations for further treatment of trees and shrubs located within the study area. In the plan, each of the trees and shrubs is assigned to an appropriate category: to retain, to maintain, to transplant, to remove, to assess, and others. The plan is prepared for the needs of a new investment requiring maintenance of the tree stand, removals or transplanting. In the case of investment planning, the plan identifies trees and shrubs conflicting with the design and construction works, and includes guidelines for the manner of conducting works within the roots, trunk and crown (e.g. using rope techniques in the crown).

We also prepare dendrology management plans and inventories in accordance with the Ordinance of the President of Wrocław on the protection of trees and development of green areas of Wrocław of 11 August 2016. We are able to prepare full documentation of the dendrology management plan with an analysis of avoidance of collisions of the planned investment with the existing tree stand, together with the preparation in QGIS Desktop of Shapefile files with the location of trees and shrubs for the SIP and correctly entered descriptive data in the required xls structure.

## Plans for historic areas

In the case of preparing a tree stand management design for a dendrology management plan for trees growing in historic areas in accordance with Art. 37a of the Act of 23 July 2003 on monument protection and care of monuments (Journal of Laws 2022.0.840), the works and documentation may be carried out by a person who "has completed second-cycle studies or uniform master's studies, in the field of conservation and restoration of works of art or monument conservation and who, after starting second-cycle studies or after passing the sixth semester of uniform master's studies, for at least 9 months participated in conservation works, restoration works or conservation research, conducted for monuments entered in the register, the inventory of a museum being a cultural institution or classified into one of the categories referred to in Art. 14a List of Heritage Treasures sec. 2." These requirements are met, inter alia, by Pracownia Architektury Krajobrazu IKROPKA Dominika Krop – Andrzejczuk, operating throughout Poland.
