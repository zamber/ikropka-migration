On 04.03.2026, on the website of the Zarząd Dróg Miejskich in Warszawa, a post entitled "Okrzei na nowo. O zieleni" was published, describing the comprehensive redevelopment of Okrzei Street carried out by us.


### Can a street in the city centre function as a "green sponge"? Zarząd Dróg Miejskich in Warszawa published a post about our project!

We proudly share the ZDM publication, which describes in detail our greenery concept for Okrzei Street. This is confirmation for us that a modern approach to landscape architecture is not only about aesthetics, but above all about advanced engineering and care for the ecosystem.

At the IKROPKA studio, we treat greenery as an equal element of urban infrastructure. How did we transfer this vision to the ground in Praga, Warszawa?

What distinguishes this project? (Key solutions for local governments and designers):

- **Nature Based Solutions (NBS) and Bio-groups** – instead of traditional tree avenues, we designed multi-layered plant clusters with a diverse species composition. This solution increases water retention, supports urban biodiversity and enhances plant resilience to urban stress.
- **Technology in the service of roots** – with dense underground infrastructure, we applied, among others, anti-compression systems, structural soils and suspended pavement structures. This ensures appropriate space for tree development without compromising surface stability.
- **Priority for existing trees** – the investment places strong emphasis on preserving current greenery. We developed a rigorous dendrology management plan and introduced strict guidelines for tree protection, including advanced arborist supervision and monitoring during works.
- **Street as an "urban sponge"** – the introduced vegetation is intended to genuinely lower the ambient temperature, reduce noise and store rainwater, creating a space friendly to residents and resilient to climate change.

Designing within the historic fabric of the city, under rigorous fire safety constraints and dense site utilities, is a demanding task. We are glad that our approach – combining sound dendrological knowledge with climate change adaptation strategy – has been recognised by the investor.

However, this is not only a road project. It is also a green space design that is to genuinely change the quality of life in this part of the city.

### Greenery not only for aesthetics

The redevelopment of ul. Okrzei is more than a new surface and organised traffic. We want a typical communication street to become a full-fledged public space – with calmed traffic and places to rest. At the centre of this change is greenery. Not as an "eye candy" addition, but as an equal element of the entire infrastructure. Trees and shrubs have a major role to play here: to improve the microclimate, provide shade, retain water, clean the air and build this part of the city's resilience to the effects of climate change.

### We care for what already grows

Before we thought about new plantings, we focused on what is already there. To take care of the existing tree stand, the greenery designers planned: widening of tree pits and loosening of the surface, application of anti-compression systems enabling root development beneath the hardened surface, use of structural soils and improvement of soil quality, protection of tree crowns and execution of necessary technical cuts, specialist tree diagnostics before and after the works, multi-year monitoring of tree condition, dendrological/arborist supervision during project implementation.

These measures are to ensure the durability of the greenery and limit the risk of its deterioration during construction works.

### New plantings. The challenge of underground networks

The design of new plantings was developed in close cooperation with the guidelines of Zarząd Zieleni m.st. Warszawy. It also had to be taken into account that in the city centre every metre underground is already occupied. Dense underground infrastructure, requirements to maintain clearance and visibility triangles, and fire safety regulations – all of this strongly limits design freedom.

Instead of a rigid, rhythmic tree layout, a more flexible solution was adopted. In a word – the plantings are adapted to what happens underground.

The key assumption was the application of nature-based solutions (Nature Based Solutions) and the introduction of the concept of so-called bio-groups – i.e. compact, multi-layered clusters of trees and shrubs of varied height and species composition. This type of compositions: increase plant resilience to wind, drought and pests, favour water retention and mitigation of the urban heat island effect, create ecological corridors for birds and small mammals, raise the level of biodiversity.

Species with high tolerance to urban stress were applied. Resistant to drought, salination and high temperatures, while adapted to limited soil space and shallow root system.

Along the green belt, rest zones with benches and neighbourhood micro-enclaves surrounded by greenery were designed. Here, the greenery is not only to grow, but to create a place to stop.

### Technology in the service of greenery

To provide plants with the best possible conditions in the difficult urban environment, the designers proposed a range of technical solutions: aeration and aeration-irrigation systems, anti-compression systems enabling root development under roadways and pavements, root screens protecting technical infrastructure, suspended pavement structures, underground tree stabilisation systems without traditional staking.

These solutions are to ensure that the greenery has a chance to settle on Okrzei for many years.

### Street like an urban sponge

The designed vegetation groups will function as the so-called urban green sponge. A system that supports retention of stormwater, improves microclimate and air quality. The greenery on ul. Okrzei will be tasked with: lowering the ambient temperature during heatwaves, storing and gradually releasing water, reducing air pollution and noise, increasing the comfort of staying in public space, positively affecting the health and well-being of residents.

Our goal is to create a resilient and resident-friendly space that will serve both current and future generations of Warszawiaks.
