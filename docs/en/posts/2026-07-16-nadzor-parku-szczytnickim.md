Nature supervision – dendrological and entomological supervision over tree removal of a tree inhabited by protected insect species.


Recently we carried out nature supervision - dendrological and entomological supervision over the removal of a tree (silver linden), growing in Park Szczytnicki in Wrocław. The tree removal was necessary for safety reasons.

In this case, the permit for removal was issued by the Municipal Monument Conservator (the area is entered in the register of monuments).


Due to the fact that the tree was inhabited by protected insect species: the hermit beetle and the great capricorn beetle, an appropriate decision of the Regional Directorate for Environmental Protection in Wrocław was also obtained to destroy the habitat of protected species and the relevant exemptions from the prohibitions resulting from the Nature Conservation Act.

The conditions of the RDOŚ decision obligated to carry out the removal under entomological supervision. The contractor of the works invited our company to carry out supervisory activities. The works were conducted under the direction of the dendrology supervision inspector mgr inż. arch. kraj. Dominiki Krop-Andrzejczuk in cooperation with the nature supervision inspector, entomologist dr. inż. Marcin Kadejem. The tree was removed by an experienced team of arborists – in sections. Each fragment of the woody debris was carefully checked for colonization by protected insects. The larvae of the hermit beetle and the great capricorn beetle were collected and transferred to a place enabling their further development. The tree hollow was properly secured and transported together with a part of the tree to a location agreed with the site manager – a Natura 2000 area, enabling the completion of the development of the developmental forms of protected insect species. We trust that the mission will end in full success, giving nature in the near future another generation of adult forms capable of undertaking reproduction. A complicated operation, but very satisfying.
