The study area is a fragment of an island on the Bystrzyca river, used by the Hydroelectric Power Plant, and a part of the riparian area south of the river channel. The study was prepared to expand fish migration facilities.


## Project description

The study area is the island area managed by the Power Plant; it is excluded from general access, and therefore this place bears the characteristics of a primeval forest. Forest management occurs through natural processes in which humans do not intervene – aging trees die, and the decomposing wood enriches the soil and becomes a feeding and habitat site for many organisms. Above the densely overgrown island tower enormous poplars, oaks, willows and black locusts.


## Challenges

The tree inventory was carried out in unfavorable weather conditions. We worked in heavy rain, sheltering ourselves and the soaking documentation sheets under umbrellas, yet difficult field work conditions are a standard for IKROPKA, which we handle excellently!


## Results

Within the island area indicated by the Client, a total of 85 trees of the stand were inventoried. The largest measured girths were: willow – 530 cm, oak – 404 cm and poplar 335 cm.
