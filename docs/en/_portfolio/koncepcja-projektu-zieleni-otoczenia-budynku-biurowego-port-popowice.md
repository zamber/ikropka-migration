Green space design - sensory garden in the surroundings of the Port Popowice office building in Wrocław. Concept authors: D. Krop-Andrzejczuk, A. Gierko. Greenery visualizations: J. Kowalik. Architecture visualizations: WXCA - Pracownia Architektury.


## Project description

The building for which the green space design was created is an office building forming part of a multifunctional residential-commercial complex. The functional program of the investment is intended to encourage users to engage in active recreation, which is to be influenced, among other things, by the proximity of riverside areas and parks: Zachodni and Popowicki. The building is situated on a raised parking structure – on the northern and western sides, the external walls are concealed by embankments with terraced greenery forms that soften the elevation of the building. On the eastern side there is a square, diversified by mound-like embankments with tree plantings as well as perennial and ornamental grass plantings. The central square will be a meeting place for office users, but also a nodal point on the route home for the residents of the estate. In creating the concept, the idea of a sensory garden was adopted, in which the composition is intended to act upon the senses. In the central square, trees were planted on raised mounds, in appropriately prepared chamber systems that will allow large trees to survive on the roof slab of the underground garage.


## Concept and plant selection

The leitmotif of the concept, referring to the color scheme of the facade and the general character of the building, is an evocation of golden, silver and brown colors. Appropriate color schemes of perennials, shrubs and trees were applied around the building according to this key. For brown shades, feather reed grass, switch grass and red hot poker were used in the selection; for silver: lamb's ear, Faassen's catmint and blue fescue; and for gold: honey locust cultivar 'Sunburst', feather grass and black-eyed Susan. The plant selection, beyond aesthetic considerations, was dictated by functionality: the chosen plants are resistant to water deficits and periodic droughts.

The layout of greenery around the building was composed so as to blur straight lines and shapes. The selected plant species create a space referring to natural areas through the use of free planting arrangements and soft forms. Multi-stem shrubs – Tatarian maples and common hazels – will complement the strip of existing greenery at the base of the slope. The greenery will favor rest in the surroundings of the building.
