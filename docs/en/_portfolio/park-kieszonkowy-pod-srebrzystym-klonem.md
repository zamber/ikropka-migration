The site development project for the pocket park Pod Srebrzystym Klonem is an example of arranging a previously unappealing space behind garage buildings. A site survey conducted prior to commencing design work revealed that the area is overgrown with large, unplanned trees and covered with patches of lawn interrupted by numerous informal paths. A significant problem was car parking taking place under tree crowns, which had to be eliminated through spatial arrangement measures. During the design process, as part of consultations with the site manager – Zarząd Zasobu Komunalnego we Wrocławiu, the design also had to be adapted to the changing function of a plot fragment, which is now to serve as access to garages.


## Project description

The overriding design assumption was the protection of existing trees, and in particular a magnificent silver maple, whose roots are visibly almost entirely within the crown projection area.


## Solutions

Therefore, the project decided on the execution of wooden ramp walkways, which help protect the tree, as they do not require the same scope of earthworks as paths executed in the ground. An additional advantage is the possibility of shifting the anchoring elements, should earthworks disturb the root system. The walkway elements and two wooden terraces can also be easily dismantled if necessary.

For the protection of root systems, it was also decided to loosen the soil, followed by measures improving the microclimate around the trees. Under the crowns, ground-cover plants will be planted and mulching will be performed, i.e. covering the soil with wood chips, which retain moisture in the plant surroundings. The walkways will organize the chaotically running informal paths across the area. On the two terraces, recreational spots with benches and wooden planters were arranged. Under the tree crowns, urban loungers were placed in a free layout, to provide users with the possibility of choosing a seating place. The presence of already existing, mature trees is an exceptional advantage, as future park users will be able to take shelter in their shade on hot days. This also affects the need to adapt the species selection of plants to conditions of almost constant shading – perennials such as hostas or ferns and periwinkle shrubs will easily withstand it.


## Results

All design work was continuously consulted with Zarząd Zieleni Miejskiej we Wrocławiu and the Project Leader. As part of the contract, the necessary permits from ZDiUM, ZZK, WUOZ and MKZ we Wrocławiu were also obtained.
