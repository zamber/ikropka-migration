The subject of the study was a tree inventory of the municipal cemetery in Prudnik together with a tree management plan as part of the revitalization of the tree stand.


## Project description

The cemetery, covering an area of nearly six hectares, is an object entered in the register of monuments. In the composition of the historic avenue layout, small-leaved lindens predominate, with an admixture of large-leaved lindens. The largest measured trunk circumference was 367 cm, and the age of the oldest tree is 150 years.

In the 1970s or 1980s, pollarding treatments were carried out on most of the trees, shortening them to a height of about 7-8 meters. The current tree crowns are coppice crowns formed as a result of these treatments. The trees are a habitat for numerous avifauna – bird nests were observed in almost all tree crowns; the activity of species such as the great tit or the Eurasian nuthatch was observed.


## Dendrochronological analysis

The additionally conducted dendrochronological analysis presents an interesting picture. Lindens over 100 years old constitute only 7% of the tree stand, while 37% are trees planted still during the German times. Over 50% are trees aged 40-70 years.
