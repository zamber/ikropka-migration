The study was prepared to assess safety in the vicinity of trees. The trees in question grow adjacent to a built-up area, where in the immediate vicinity there is a multi-family building and a parking lot for residents.

The trees were subjected to thorough dendrological inspection. The sanitary condition of roots, trunks and crowns was examined, and the immediate surroundings of the trees were analyzed, verifying habitat conditions.


## Project Description

Analysis of available cartographic materials indicated that the trees have probably grown under unchanged conditions since planting. Prior to the construction of the multi-family building on the site in question, there was a parking lot with a greenery strip, which currently forms a strip along the northern elevation of the building. Its width has probably not changed.

The health condition of the poplar trees was determined as good, posing no threat to people and property. Minor care interventions were recommended, i.e. removal of fine deadwood and mistletoe, as well as tree monitoring every two years.

Additionally, due to the close proximity, the opinion also covered a potentially hazardous tree located outside the Client's plot. Its condition was assessed as poor, as evidenced by symptoms of trunk infection by an aggressive fungus causing decay, the tree's structure, and numerous damages to the trunk base.
