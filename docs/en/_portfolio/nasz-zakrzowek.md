The Zakrzówek area, a former open-pit mine, is an exceptional space of Kraków. For years, the reservoir left to itself along with the surrounding areas has become a highly valuable ecological environment, adapted in many ways by the residents.

## Project Description

The extraordinary waters of the reservoir remain in use by the Diving Centre, the rocky walls have been adopted by the climbing community of Kraków, and Zakrzówek in general is a frequent place for meetings, walks or bike rides.

As part of the competition work, numerous natural, landscape, transport, functional-use and view analyses were carried out, thanks to which the vast space of Zakrzówek was divided into zones with different degrees of design intervention. The competition concept assumes the creation of a full-value public space using pro-ecological and pro-natural innovations, which will become a safe place willingly visited not only by the residents of Kraków, but also by tourists.

On the water reservoir, floating pools with wooden recreational piers were created, as well as a footbridge connecting the two sides of the reservoir at the narrows. For the needs of divers, changing rooms were designed – temporary roof structures in a modern form transitioning in places into the surface and elements of landscape furniture. On the high banks, paths secured with railings were designed, diversified with places for open-air bonfires and viewpoints. Throughout the entire area, several walking loops were led, a visual identification concept was created and a railway route was established. In order to preserve biologically valuable habitats, in the natural part of the paths they were designed in the form of elevated wooden platforms, protecting the ground from being trampled. The remaining paths are permeable mineral surfaces.

In cooperation with architects, designs of several buildings were created, including a waterside restaurant, the Education Centre and sanitary facilities. In the designed buildings, modern ecological technologies reducing operating costs were used, such as solar and photovoltaic panels, rainwater harvesting and others. At the Education Centre, a sensory garden of senses and an ecological education garden as well as a playground for children were designed. A waterside restaurant with a glazed façade and a beautiful view of the reservoir was also designed.

In the part used by climbers, design activities were minimized to preserve the naturalistic landscape of the surroundings. The location of an outdoor gym was indicated, the railway loop was marked out and new waterside climbing walls were designed on the northern wall of the reservoir.

In the part located not far from single-family and multi-family housing estates, new sports functions were designated: a bike park, a skatepark, a basketball and volleyball court, and a marina with a floating restaurant on the planned relief channel. The entire area was equipped with parking lots and sanitary pavilions.
