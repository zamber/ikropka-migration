A concept for a forest garden of naturalistic character fitting into the landscape, among others through the preservation of existing trees and forest floor vegetation.

As part of the study, a tree inventory was carried out together with a tree stand management design, in which a large part of the existing plants were designated for transplanting within the study area. Analysing the character of the garden and the client's wishes, a composition of visual openings was designed – glades enveloped by densely wooded areas. A construction project for paths and landscape furniture elements was prepared.


## Project description

To make the garden accessible for a disabled user moving in a wheelchair, two loops of hardened paths were designed, along which a sensory garden in raised planters and a worktop in the utility part were provided. These elements are adapted in height and construction to the needs of a disabled person.

The representative part of the garden is located in front of the main entrance to the house from the western side. Here, only a few plant species planted in larger groups were used, thus creating a legible composition. Among others, the following were applied: groundcover rose, mountain pine, lavender and Japanese blood grass. The entrance to the house is flanked by shrubs of garden hydrangea.

In the recreational part of the garden, a wooden terrace of irregular form was designed around the trunk of a tree. Perennial beds and ornamental planters were adapted in shape and form to the course of the main path.

The utility part of the garden is located on the eastern side. Grapevine growing on a trellis by the garage building and part of the fruit trees were preserved. For vegetable cultivation, raised wooden planters were designed: two as boxes and one adapted to a wheelchair with a worktop. The planters are situated on a circular layout on a gravel surface. In the representative and recreational parts of the garden, several lighting points were planned, whose light will emphasize the beauty of the garden after dusk.
