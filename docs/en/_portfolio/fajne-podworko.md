Green space design in a courtyard interior in Wrocław with an area of 7937 m².
The task was to create a coherent greenery composition integrating the entire courtyard,
enriching the residents' space and adapted to difficult usage conditions.


## Project description

The design assumption was to achieve coherent greenery with an integrating character for the entire courtyard.
Greenery compositions play a significant role in the views from residents' windows, accompany architectural
elements and site equipment such as playgrounds, benches, and also constitute screening greenery
by the waste bins in the courtyard. The design proposed tree and shrub species that significantly enhance
the aesthetic values of the designed space. The species used are plants resistant to difficult conditions,
such as trampling.


## Plant composition

The layout of the designed vegetation corresponds with the functional arrangement of the site and the location
of parking spaces. Species such as maidenhair tree (*Ginkgo biloba*) significantly
influence the quality of the plant cover in the courtyard interior. Tall greenery also serves a screening function here,
e.g. a row of rowans conceals the waste bin shelter located in the central part of the site.
Deciduous shrubs complement the composition.


## Implementation

We provided investor supervision over the implementation.
The project was created on commission from the architectural office Grupa projektowa Mieszko Mazurek KONCEPT.
