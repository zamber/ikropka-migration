The study aimed to assess the health condition of the natural monument – the Włost's Oak in Wrocław – prior to the commencement of investment works in the public spaces adjacent to the tree within the historic WUWA housing estate, entered in the register of monuments.


## Description of the expert assessment

The dendrology expert assessment is a document specifying recommendations concerning the protection of the tree in connection with the planned investment, including those regarding its securing during the construction period and after its completion. It also includes an analysis of the site development project in the vicinity of the oak.

The historical background of the tree in question was also analyzed by interpreting archival maps. They indicate that the Włost's Oak is probably part of an avenue planting along the main road leading to the Zielony Dąb (Grueneiche) manor.


## Research and recommendations

During field studies, detailed dendrological inspections of the tree in question were carried out. Basic dendrometric measurements were performed and its health condition was described in detail. The girth of the Piotr Włost's Oak measured 520 cm, and its health condition was determined as good. A series of care treatments was recommended, including the dismantling of the too-small fence surrounding the tree trunk, which damages root flares, the excavation of the mounded root collar, the thinning and relief of parts of the tree crown, as well as the removal of deadwood and the inspection of bindings.


## Outcomes

The outcome of the analysis of the site development project in the vicinity of the oak was the preparation of a sequence project for surface demolitions and the establishment of a tree protection zone.
