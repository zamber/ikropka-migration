The green space design at the Profit Plaza complex, in accordance with the provisions of the Local Spatial Development Plan, takes the form of a promenade and scenic boulevard along the river. The LSPDP guidelines mandated the design of a pedestrian and cycling route, which was fulfilled by designating communication as the main function.

## Project Description

The relatively narrow strip of land designated for the promenade was, in its remaining part, developed with high and low greenery as well as landscape furniture. The existing high greenery, previously inventoried, was taken into account in the design.

Along the promenade, a single-species avenue was planted in a double row of planter – bench systems additionally filled with low vegetation. The species composition of the avenue includes: red maple, which complements the light facade of the designed building, Gautier's fescue forming attractive, soft cushions, appealing in combination with the texture of the wooden bench, and small-flowered heuchera in the crimson cultivar 'Palace Purple' referencing the maples.

The boulevard sequence is terminated by a sculpture surrounded by mock oranges, placed on the main viewing axis of the boulevard and the second avenue leading towards ul. Kurkowej. The second avenue was planted with spherical-form maples, and instead of a lawn, small-flowered heuchera was used.

The use of contrasting and complementary colors (red – green) in appropriate proportions allows for diversity, all the more so as the vegetation used in the design possesses many qualities: it colors in autumn (red maples, Virginia creeper), is evergreen (cherry laurels and Gautier's fescues), and has an attractive scent (fragrant mock oranges surrounding the sculpture), which undoubtedly constitutes an additional attraction of this area.

Along the pedestrian and cycling routes, in order not to suffocate the tree roots, the use of permeable paving is necessary. An ideal solution in such cases is the Terraway-type permeable paving, which positively affects the general condition, health status, and vitality of trees. An additional advantage of this paving is that it prevents overgrowth – it is resistant to the germination of grass and weed seeds. For the existing tree with inventory number 22, a root zone grating around the trunk was designed to enable free circulation under changed conditions after the completed investment.
