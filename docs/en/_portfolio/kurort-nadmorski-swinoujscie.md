A concept submitted to the competition for the urban and architectural design concept for the development of public areas in the Coastal District "Seaside Resort Świnoujście – a new vision of public space".


## Project description

In the project, a new public space was created based on the character of the natural landscape of the West Pomeranian Baltic coast. Elements of dune greenery as well as landscape furniture and architectural detail referring to maritime traditions were introduced into the interior of the district.

Pedestrian and pedestrian-cycling routes were designed providing the opportunity for contact with the natural landscape, with the Baltic fauna and flora. The main seaside passage was enriched with service, cultural and recreational functions, creating local centres of varied character. The square in front of the bandstand was supplemented with an attraction in the form of multimedia water organs, benches, and multi-species vegetation.

The promenade was designed in reference to the adjacent dune forest – the avenue along the promenade is a mixed avenue, its rhythm is variable as in a natural environment. The dune forest enters the area of the hardened square "Resort Forum", with the principle of thinning, ordering and selection of species. The design concept provides for the preservation of historic greenery, the ordering of greenery in terms of sanitary and compositional aspects – ensuring safety for users and revealing the main view axes of the seaside landscape, thus showing its greatest assets – the sea and the seaside, natural landscape. The designed vegetation adapts to the existing character of seaside vegetation.

The greenery was planned using new technologies for trees (innovations for the environment) i.e. structural soil, or suspended ramp walkways (in order to avoid damage to root systems).
