The site development was applied to a small garden (92 m²) located at the back of the house. Previously, the garden had not been subjected to care treatments. The only plants present in this part of the plot were two relatively young trees.

According to the investor's expectations, the garden was to serve mainly recreational and decorative functions – for this purpose, a garden terrace design was also created, acting as a kind of "stage" for admiring the garden. A request to separate a small utility space was also made, which was included in the form of a small paved area with a hardened surface by the terrace steps.


## Garden design

In the decorative part of the garden, it was decided to minimize hardened surfaces at the expense of maximizing biologically active areas – in the form of turf and designed vegetation. Thanks to this measure, the area gained a clear division into recreational and utility space. The recreational part is divided into a leisure area, whose function is performed by the terrace, and a decorative one performed by the rest of the garden.

Since the developed area is relatively small, it was decided to use few species to avoid introducing unnecessary chaos. The decorative garden was designed in the form of a curved arc, accentuated by border beds, which form the boundary of the lawn and plant plantings. Directly under the trees, due to significant shading, considerable losses were observed in the lawn. In order to avoid this problem in the future, ground-cover perennials were planned in its place, which with age will densify and lushly turf spaces difficult to reach with a mower. The plant elements of the garden were "interwoven" with small gravel surfaces. The principle of multi-layering was applied here. Despite the small dimensions of the space covered by the project, an effort was made to diversify it as much as possible. Almost every element here is a background for the next one – this creates an interesting composition, attractive to view from the terrace and the house windows.


## Solutions

The investor's wish was to place at least the smallest water element on the property – which was fulfilled in the form of a mini fountain – a "wet stone".


## Effect

The project was completed.
