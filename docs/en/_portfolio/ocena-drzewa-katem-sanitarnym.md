The subject of the study was the preparation of a dendrology opinion for a natural monument – a small-leaved lime (Tilia cordata) with a breast-height circumference of 416 cm, growing on the premises of a kindergarten playground. The dendrology opinion aimed to assess the sanitary condition of the tree and evaluate the safety in its surroundings, as well as to issue recommendations for its further management.


## Description of the Study

The assessment of the tree at the playground was carried out during the leaf-on period. Detailed dendrological examinations of the tree were conducted. Dendrological inspection was performed, along with dendrometric measurements of the circumference at several heights, diameter and crown spread in four directions, as well as tree height.

In describing the health status, particular attention was paid to the condition of the root system; the immediate surroundings of the tree were verified, noting any damage and irregularities. The condition of the trunk (mechanical strength, potential defects, damage) and crown (presence of deadwood, mistletoe, leaf diseases, branch condition) was assessed.

An analysis of the tree's surroundings over the years was also carried out through a review of historical topographic maps.


## Conclusions and Recommendations

The conclusions from the tree examination allowed for the determination of the sanitary condition of the natural monument and the assessment of safety around the tree. Recommendations were issued within the framework of the tree stand management project in two scenarios, leading to ensuring safety in the tree's surroundings.
