The subject of the study is a design concept together with a series of analyses and a functional-spatial program for the entirety of the park, being part of the green areas of the so-called Southern Wedge of Wrocław. The concept takes into account the context of functional-spatial connections with other green areas within the Wedge: Skowroni Park, areas of allotment gardens and sports grounds at ul. Spiska, and the Gen. Anders Hill. The concept served as the basis for further design works, including the development of the construction and technical project for the playground for older children, the obstacle course, and the recreational glade under this task.


## Project Description

The study area is located in Wrocław between ul. Borowska, ul. Kamienna, ul. Ślężna, and ul. Jacka Kuronia. It is situated between two residential areas: Śródmieście Południowe and Huby, and constitutes a fragment of the Southern Wedge of Wrocław. Historically, the area of the present Park served a cemetery function: maps from the 1930s show the cemeteries of St. Mary Magdalene (in the western part), St. Maurice (in the eastern part of the park), and St. Dorothy (with a cemetery avenue and chapel, in the center of the study area). The cemeteries were liquidated in the 1950s–1960s, and the area was developed into a park.

The main assumptions of the concept were:
- calming of traffic, delineation of main walking loops;
- creation of a buffer separating the study area from the transformer station and the tram depot at ul. Kamienna;
- preservation and emphasis of the historical planting layout, including highlighting the relic linden avenues – remnants of the former cemetery;
- narrative character of the park referring to its hero – Gen. W. Anders and his army;
- improvement of safety and comfort of use conditions, taking into account the transport connection with the green areas belonging to the Southern Wedge of Wrocław;
- attractive functional-spatial program for all users.


## Design Concept

Preservation and emphasis of the historical planting layout consists in referring, in the compositional layout of the park, to the orthogonal, historical arrangement of the former cemeteries. The main promenade was treated as the avenue running in the north-south direction, which begins with a square being the remnant of the cemetery chapel. In the square, it was decided to plant trained lindens, creating green volumetric objects from them. They are to refer to the architecture of the chapel. The historical paths running perpendicular from the main avenue were distinguished by covering them with natural surface, a layer of bark and wood chips. Some of them are part of the adventure trail in the park.

The aim of the project is to give the park a narrative character referring to the park's patron – General Władysław Anders. It is planned to preserve the forest "climate" of the park and maintain activity areas in a simple, wooden style, referring to camp pioneering. The inspiration for creating the park hero became the bear Wojtek – a bear adopted by soldiers of the 22nd Artillery Supply Company of the 2nd Polish Corps commanded by Gen. Władysław Anders. The bear took part in the battle of Monte Cassino and even received the rank of corporal. In the park he was commemorated with a monument with a dwarf, while in the concept of the "Adventure Park in Anders Park" he was given the name of a narrator who guides users through the entire park area, involving them in various puzzles and tasks to perform. Thanks to Wojtek, every visit to the park will become a true adventure!

The program of Anders Park refers to the scouting and guiding method, which combines play and game, providing comprehensive development and character shaping through challenges. It was divided into 4 thematic paths, named trails:
- Toddler Play Trail – an enclosed playground for the youngest with a path for free play marked out with natural elements (boulders, stones, stumps, logs). Around the path, play equipment adapted to the age group (swings, rockers and carousel, wooden platform and seating) was located. The play will be diversified by games and curiosities placed on bear boards.
- Cub Adventure Trail – in the playground, rotating and jumping equipment was placed, a carousel with suspended seats, training users' muscles. In the central part, it was decided to leave the existing slide and swing, and the most valuable backyard attraction was not forgotten either – a trzepak, in the form of gymnastic bars (triple braid). The second part of the cub trail is a rope park together with an inclusive part – adapted to the needs of children with disabilities – in which a triple integrative swing was placed. The equipment on the playground was placed in the form of trails, which is to encourage children to explore a larger part of the park. The signposts of this park adventure will be bear boards with tasks and games placed on them. For tired cubs, hammocks among trees, seats and platforms were designed.
- Tracker (Performance) Obstacle Trail – for ambitious athletes and calisthenics enthusiasts, an obstacle trail was designed. It contains a high diagonal balance beam, a "butterfly" net, a 3-meter ladder, and a climbing ramp. Selected equipment is intended for advanced users who want to improve their fitness and motor coordination.
- Health Trail – the health loop consists of two activity spots with fitness equipment encouraging walks around the entire loop in the park. The first spot is located in the southern part of the park and was enriched with a rehabilitation path with gymnastic handrails under which it is proposed to lay a sensory surface. The second is located in the western part of the park, between the historical paths. The equipment placed in the loops offers simple, general-development training.

Moreover, a neighborhood (recreational) glade was designed, which will become an ideal place for shared, family time. It was decided to leave a free grassy glade (area 1626.15 m²) placing on it two scenic, large swings set on the axis of the study area. An additional common space is the family square, equipped with picnic tables and a boules play trail.


## Greenery in Anders Park

The whole layout is axial in character, based on the meridian course of the white horse chestnut avenue in the western part of the park. The avenue constitutes the main pedestrian route ensuring communication between ul. Kamienna and ul. Jacka Kuronia. The greenery layout in the park is free in character. The exception are the rows and relics of former avenues, which rhythmically mark the course of the former cemetery quarters. High vegetation dominates in the park area. The tree stand can mostly be classified as mature with a small admixture of young plantings.

The planned plantings aim to create a buffer zone, separating the recreational areas from the power station, the tram depot, and the 5th General Secondary School. Plantings of coniferous and deciduous shrubs are proposed here. High trees were designed on the created squares and along the bicycle path led along ul. J. Kuronia. The proposed perennial beds constitute a complement to the surface layout of the playgrounds (play, obstacle, adventure and health trails). They are maintained in a forest character. In the squares at ul. J. Kuronia, oval beds in raised planters were introduced, fitting into the layout of irregularly distributed benches.
