A home garden design of approx. 1400 m² in the village of Krzyków near Wrocław, in accordance with the investor's requirements, provides for site development in a manner that ensures the lowest possible maintenance effort during subsequent care. At the same time, it is not monotonous and is not limited exclusively to the use of evergreen coniferous vegetation.


## Project Description

This is a garden for recreational use by an elderly couple, traditional in form, ultimately easy to maintain.

The design mainly employs trees and shrubs, complemented by groundcover plants, dwarf shrubs and perennial perennials. Annual or biennial plants requiring more attention were omitted. Plant species were selected so that the garden remains attractive throughout the year. Thus, one can observe the variability across successive seasons. Plant colours change, scents change, and the view from the window and terrace changes. In selecting plants, the designers considered the Clients' colour preferences and their favourite plants, to which they are sentimentally attached for certain reasons – these include roses, lavender, pyracantha and birches. As a result, the garden acquired a romantic character, further emphasised by beds of delicate, arching shapes.


## Solutions

All client suggestions were taken into account in the design process. Both the composition relative to the Investor's house and the neighbouring plots was important. Particular attention was paid to future views from the house windows – therefore, plant compositions in front of the windows form "frames" for further perspectives, building attractive scenes during moments spent inside the house. The north-eastern corner of the plot was designated, per the investor's requirement, for a small oval terrace intended as a barbecue area. It is accompanied by a screen in the form of climbers on supports, delineating an intimate nook free from neighbours' views. The central part of the southern section of the garden consists of an "island" with a multi-tier planting arrangement. This is simultaneously the main dominant of the garden, providing a screen for the septic tank.
