The project was developed based on the concept and the functional-spatial program prepared in the first stage of works by PAK IKROPKA. The study covers the central fragment of Park Andersa in Wrocław with an area of 10 133 m², for which we developed a detailed construction and technical project taking into account four main activity zones.


## Project description

The project provides for the extension and reconstruction of a fragment of recreational areas in Park Andersa. The recreational areas were divided into four functional zones: a playground with an adventure trail in the western part of the park, an outdoor gym being part of the performance trail in its eastern part, and in the central strip: a recreational glade with viewing swings and a family glade with a pétanque court and picnic tables. The children's playground was created in place of an already existing playground: the area was cleaned up, play equipment that was no longer fit for use was inventoried and removed, and new equipment was designed. The playground area was diversified with a gravel path with benches and edges made of natural materials – fieldstones, black locust logs, trunks – as well as plantings: forest perennials and shrubs. Benches and bins were replaced with new ones, and bicycle racks were added by the picnic benches and the performance trail. Information boards and information posts are an element of the park's identity contained in the design concept. The content placed in them constitutes a field game whose route runs through the fragment of the park covered by the project. Navigation posts indicate the direction of movement along a given route, which will be developed at further stages of the park's implementation. Tree plantings complement the existing avenues and also emphasize the geometric layout of the recreational strip. Numerous perennial beds supplement the site development.

The scope of the study includes the following works:
- Demolition of landscape furniture and site development elements.
- Renovation of the communication system and surface hardening.
- Placement of landscape furniture.
- Arrangement of greenery.

The aim of the project is to give the park a **narrative character** referring to the park's patron – General Władysław Anders. It is planned to preserve the forest "climate" of the park and maintain activity areas in a simple, wooden style referring to camp pioneering. The inspiration to create the park hero was **Wojtek the bear** – a bear adopted by the soldiers of the 22nd Artillery Supply Company of the 2nd Polish Corps commanded by Gen. Władysław Anders. The bear took part in the Battle of Monte Cassino and even received the rank of corporal. In the park he was commemorated with a monument with a dwarf, while in the concept of "Adventure Park in Park Andersa" he was given the name of a narrator who guides users through the entire park area involving them in various puzzles and tasks to perform. Thanks to Wojtek, every visit to the park will become a true adventure!

The program of Park Andersa refers to the scout and guide method, which combines play and game, ensuring versatile development and character building through challenges. It was divided into 4 thematic paths, named trails:

- **Toddler play trail** – an enclosed playground for the youngest with a path for free play marked out with natural elements (boulders, stone stumps, logs). Around the path, play equipment adapted to the age group was placed (swings, rockers and carousel, wooden platform and seat). The play will be diversified by games and curiosities placed on bear boards.

- **Cub adventure trail** – on the playground, rotating and jumping equipment was placed, a carousel with suspended seats, training users' muscles. In the central part it was decided to leave the existing slide and swing, and not to forget the most valuable backyard attraction – a "trzepak" (clothes-beating frame), in the form of gymnastic bars (triple braid). The second part of the cub trail is a rope park together with an inclusive part – adapted to the needs of children with disabilities – in which a triple integrative swing was placed. The equipment on the playground was placed in the form of trails, which is to encourage children to explore a larger part of the park. The signposts of this park adventure will be bear boards with tasks and games placed on them. For tired cubs, hammocks among trees, seats and platforms were designed.

- **Tracker obstacle trail (performance)** – for ambitious athletes and calisthenics enthusiasts, an obstacle trail was designed. It contains a high diagonal balance beam, a "butterfly" net, a 3-metre ladder and a climbing ramp. Selected equipment is intended for advanced users who want to improve their fitness and motor coordination.

- **Health trail** – the health loop consists of two activity spots with fitness equipment encouraging walks around the entire loop in the park. The first spot is located in the southern part of the park and was enriched with a rehabilitation path with gymnastic handrails under which it is proposed to lay a sensory surface. The second is located in the western part of the park, between the historic alleys. The equipment placed in the loops offers simple, general-development training.

Moreover, a **neighbourhood (recreational) glade** was designed, which will become an ideal place for joint, family time spending. It was decided to leave a free grassy glade (area 1626.15 m²) placing on it two large viewing swings set on the axis of the developed area. An additional common space is the **family square**, equipped with picnic tables and a pétanque court.


## Challenges

The specificity of the project required numerous arrangements, including with the Municipal Monument Conservator, the Provincial Office for Monument Protection, the Bureau of Sustainable Mobility, as well as with the operators of underground infrastructure networks – among others, a high-voltage cable line runs through the park.


## Greenery in Park Andersa

In the green space design for the subject task, native species adapted to the site dominate. The entire greenery layout creates a strongly naturalistic composition based on rounded, soft lines and shrub clusters. Among the designed trees we can distinguish field maple, manna ash, white mulberry and small-leaved lime. Among the shrub layer, worthy of attention are: black elder, guelder rose or mock orange. The undergrowth constitutes rich ground cover based on groundcover plants, grasses and perennials. Particularly noteworthy are geranium, yellow archangel, chestnut-leaved rodgersia, heart-leaved tiarella, snowy hairgrass or male fern.
