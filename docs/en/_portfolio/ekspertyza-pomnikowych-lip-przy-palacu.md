The expert assessment aimed to evaluate the safety of property and people in the vicinity of three trees growing adjacent to the façade of a palace designated for renovation. The trees in question – **natural monuments** – are broad-leaved lindens (*Tilia platyphyllos*) of the linden species, reaching over 25 m in height.


## Description of the Expert Assessment

The entomological expert assessment indicates that one of the trees constitutes a potential habitat for the hermit beetle (*Osmoderma eremita*) – a beetle species under strict protection in Poland.

In the field, dendrometric measurements were carried out in accordance with standards, a detailed visual tree condition assessment was conducted, and the trees were examined using a sonic tomograph, which allows determining the level and extent of wood decay inside the trunk. Furthermore, study investigations and calculations of trunk breaking strength were performed in accordance with the SIA (*Static Integrated Assessment*) method by L. Wessolly.


## Research Results

The conducted studies revealed internal trunk defects in the trees in question, which nevertheless maintain a high and good safety level in terms of resistance to trunk breakage.
