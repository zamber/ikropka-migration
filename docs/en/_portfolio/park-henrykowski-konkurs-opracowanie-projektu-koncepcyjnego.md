Designers from PAK IKROPKA in cooperation with KMP Poniewierka sp. z o.o. and AIS Śliwa Dobosz sp. z o.o. sp.k. took part in a single-stage competition for the development of a concept design for the site development of the post-railway area in the vicinity of ul. Henrykowska and ul. Paczkowska in Wrocław. The area will adopt the name "Park Henrykowski" in the future.

In 2019, as part of the Wrocław Participatory Budget edition, more than 6.3 thousand people voted for the project of the future park. During December consultations in the same year, residents expressed their views on expected solutions. As part of the competition, the task of the designers was to include the maximum number of social recommendations while preserving the original, industrial character of the place, which is a railway area.


## Design Description

The design team of PAK IKROPKA, KMP Poniewierka and AIS Śliwa Dobosz was guided in the development of the design concept by three principal guidelines:

- creation of a wild urban park through the use of the richness of existing vegetation and biodiversity, preservation of the landscape and natural values of the natural ecosystem, and actions aimed at supporting natural succession,
- creation of a place for relaxation and rest, both active and passive, for local residents across a diverse age range,
- creation of new pedestrian and cycling connections, using the natural terrain relief and leading them along the traces of former tracks, embankments and ravines, emphasizing the railway character of the park.


## Design Solutions

The designers attached great importance to natural solutions. The design phase was preceded by detailed field studies. Characteristic plants occurring in the study area include, among others, great mullein, chalk hawksbeard, Centaurea maculosa, chicory, white sweet clover, as well as trees and shrubs such as poplar, Swedish whitebeam, single-seeded hawthorn or dog rose. The main assumption of the concept is to leave and support the natural succession of existing habitats through actions such as selective removal of seedlings, and in other places abandonment of mowing. A strategy of action based on minimal intervention in the existing ecosystem was adopted.

In the selection and management of materials, principles of using existing resources, limiting intervention in the terrain, and introducing the smallest possible number of new elements into the existing development were adopted. References to the railway character of the area were planned through details of landscape furniture and the use of historical equipment elements, such as steel posts, which are to serve as orientation points on the route of the designed walking path. The existing elevation was adapted into a recreational area, designing slides on the slopes, and planning a regularly mown glade at the top, connected with the main designed pedestrian route by a light wooden-structure footbridge. The hill will remain an observation point with a view of the future park and the surroundings, and an existing road as well as additionally seasonally mown pedestrian routes will lead to its summit.
