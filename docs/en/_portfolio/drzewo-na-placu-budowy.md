As part of the study, detailed dendrological examinations of the tree in question were carried out, basic dendrometric measurements were performed (trunk circumference, exact crown diameter, height), and the health condition was described in detail; the crown projection diameter was precisely measured using a laser rangefinder.


## Project Description

The implementation of a road investment and earthworks in the close vicinity of the tree (within the root system covering the crown projection range and approx. 1.5 m beyond) usually has a negative impact on the tree, which is most often observed about 3 years after completion of the investment.


## Challenges

The narrow-leaved ash has the largest lateral root spread among all native European trees (they usually reach several to a dozen or so metres beyond the crown projection). The roots are strong, tolerate transplanting well, and are sensitive to heavily compacted soil.

The condition of the trunk was diagnosed especially in the lower part using a rubber mallet. The tree was also checked for colonization by protected species of plants, animals and fungi. Photographic documentation was prepared.

A detailed analysis of the tree's surroundings was carried out in the context of the planned investment. Earthworks conducted on the western side of the tree – a deep excavation and the associated reductions and damage to the root system do not give the tree the best prognosis.


## Solutions

Conclusions and recommendations were formulated for changing the investment design, including the recommendation to apply a root barrier and to set back the planned excavation and redesign the ramp wall, which will allow saving part of the root system. Guidelines for the protection of the tree on the construction site were prepared, as well as detailed care recommendations within the crown and root system through the application of specialized arboricultural treatments on the tree.


## Effects

Undertaking remedial actions can to a significant extent strengthen the tree and give it better chances of survival while maintaining safety.
