Transformations in the city are an endless process that requires posing new questions and seeking solutions most adequate to the prevailing situation. In the era of climate change, a new perspective on greenery becomes crucial, along with understanding and appreciating the processes occurring naturally in the natural environment. As part of the site development project for the park at ul. Idzikowskiego, we support these processes and emulate them so that urban green areas become more resilient and diverse.


## Project description

The study area is located in Wrocław between ul. Ludwika Idzikowskiego and ul. Metalowców, in the Gądów Mały district of Wrocław. Historically, the area of the present Park served as a storage site for construction materials during the construction of the Gądów Mały housing estate. Remnants of those times are fragments of concrete slabs, spoil, and foundation footings of temporary buildings visible in the terrain, which served as the base of operations for the builders of the Gądów Mały estate. The park area, left for years without significant intervention, has undergone natural environmental processes, astonishing with the biodiversity of wild nature.


## Design assumptions

The main assumptions of the project were:
- organizing paths and laying out main walking routes;
- preserving the post-construction, raw character of the place;
- the idea of park development based on construction site motifs;
- preparing the site development project and the technical project including a playground for older children, toddlers, and a dog run;
- emphasizing the natural succession processes occurring in the park and creating space for the development of fourth nature;
- the educational and experimental character of the park based on the principles of biodiversity and natural succession, and limiting intervention in ongoing natural processes.


## Solution concept

The aim of the project is to give the park a narrative character referring to the construction site and the creators of urban transformations – urban planners, builders, architects. Planned is the maintenance of activity spaces in a simple, geometric style. The process of design, struggling with physics, and manual labor was the starting point for the arrangement of playgrounds for older and younger children. Among the designed activity zones we can distinguish:
- **Construction site** – a place for play and creation. Here a huge excavator with an interior full of attractions and a sand factory will stand. The playground will also include balance and seesaw equipment, thanks to which children can feel like constructors and explore the force of gravity.
- **Toddler area** – a play space for joint swinging, jumping, sliding, and handling transport – without which nothing can be built in the city.
- **Dog run** – a safe space to play with four-legged friends.

The time has come when we must depart from the practice of constantly improving and tending every fragment of urban greenery. The time of third nature – fully controlled by man and tamed by the secateurs – has ended. Alongside new plantings in the park and mown areas, we leave room for vegetation developing spontaneously and uncontrolled – fourth nature. In the park at ul. Idzikowskiego, the green space design is carried out together with nature, because it knows best how to do it. We observe the ongoing processes, emphasize them, and develop the narrative around them. The whole concept is strongly naturalistic, even wild.


## Greenery elements

**Urban meadow**
Mowing is planned only in selected spots, which will enable the development of a meadow habitat and increase the biological quality beyond the mown areas. Glades, i.e. more frequently mown areas, are planned near recreational areas. These areas will be marked by low fences, making it easier to manage maintenance works in the park. The remaining area will spontaneously overgrow with tall vegetation, thereby creating an external isolation buffer of the park. The fences will be made of dead wood obtained on site – a valuable shelter for insects and small mammals.

**Biogroup**
Along the main north-south axis, two climatic biogroups are designed. These are separated blocks that will be overgrown with wild fruit trees supplemented by shrubs. In the era of climate and economic change, edible trees and plants constitute a valuable resource that should become not only an ornament but the basis for creating greenery. Such a shaped biogroup emulates an ecotone zone.

**Weedland**
A new perspective of residents on thickets and weeds may be one of the actions for urban adaptation to climate change. It has been proven that herbaceous vegetation on wasteland can absorb more carbon dioxide than an ordered park. The green space design provides for the introduction of non-standard beds using herbs, weeds, nectariferous plants, and natural succession species. Wild, unruly, natural – yet extremely valuable in terms of biodiversity, visual qualities, and bee benefit.

**Ruderal mounds**
Vegetation penetrating concrete surfaces, remnants of conducted construction works, is undoubtedly a distinguishing feature of the designed park. Fragments of concrete slabs from the redevelopment of the square, intended for dismantling, will form the base for new plant compositions. On the mounds, perennial beds and ruderal plant beds will be created. In some places, the overgrowing process within the park space can be observed. The sown plants will be left without further human intervention so that they penetrate the structures of the concrete mounds.

**Natural succession**
In places where the existing concrete surface is preserved, no additional plantings are planned. In the process of natural succession, the surface will gradually transform: slabs will crack, weather, and crumble as a result of, e.g., winter freezing of water. Spontaneous vegetation will appear in the crevices. Initially herbaceous plants, then woody ones – growing between the slabs. Over time, deposited organic matter will form a habitat for new, more demanding plants. In this way, nature takes over and dominates areas previously transformed by humans.

**Ruderal gallery** is an educational place where nature overgrows the remaining floors of former buildings, creating a spectacle of natural processes and showing the fortitude of every plant penetrating the concrete structure.

**Ruderal hall** is a natural interior formed by trees and shrubs around one of the concrete surfaces. Thus, an ideal space for neighborhood events was created in the park.

**Mowing**
Due to the desire to improve the microclimate and adapt the city to climate change, various maintenance methods were introduced in the park: areas of different mowing intensity were separated (intensive mowing and medium-intensive mowing). The remaining areas are to remain unmown and overgrow with tall vegetation, thereby creating an external isolation buffer of the park.
