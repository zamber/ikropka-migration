As part of the investment – the construction of the new seat of the Tax Chamber, IKROPKA prepared a dendrology expert assessment and protection plan for the most valuable tree within the study area – a London plane with a circumference of 475 cm. The fee for the removal of this tree due to conflict with the investment amounted to PLN 1 million. The investor abandoned the intention to remove it and commissioned a substitute design providing for the preservation of the tree.


## Project Description

Throughout the 2-year duration of the construction, IKROPKA provided dendrological supervision of activities and construction works across the entire construction site, with particular consideration given to the plane tree. The supervision consisted in recommending specific measures concerning the tree, overseeing the manner in which the underground garage, buildings and site development were executed, so that no construction works would negatively affect the condition of the tree and would proceed using all available means and the latest knowledge regarding tree protection on the construction site.

Pursuant to the requirements of the dendrologist inspector, the tree was irrigated, fertilized, maintained and protected in all zones (root system, trunk and crown). The root ball was secured for the entire duration of the construction against potential damage as well as against desiccation. All works were carried out under supervision.


## Results

It is assumed that after approximately 3–5 years it can be determined whether the investment had a negative impact on the condition of the trees in its surroundings. Only after such time does the tree exhibit a reaction to the stress caused by construction interference such as: the level of the groundwater table, soil compaction or root reduction within the root system, damage to the trunk or branches, improperly performed pruning of branches and limbs, and many others.

After the elapsing of the 3 test years, it can be stated with full certainty that the supervision over the plane tree at the Tax Chamber was concluded successfully! The construction of two buildings with an underground garage was completed and the beautiful, old tree was preserved. The tree is in good condition and shows no deterioration of its sanitary status. Currently, the site users – the employees of the Tax Chamber – make use of the zone by the tree, where a rest area (benches, bins) has been arranged in its shade.
