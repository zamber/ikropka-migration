Thanks to the 2017 Wrocław Participatory Budget project, concerning the implementation of pocket parks throughout the city, the triangular square at ul. W. Grabskiego will finally receive a completely new arrangement. As part of this task, PAK IKROPKA carried out a tree inventory of the area, a design concept, a building and construction project for site development in terms of surface hardening, terrain shaping, landscape furniture objects and greenery.

Currently, it is an undeveloped and unused area, serving mainly for communication purposes.

In the pre-design process, beyond performing the tree inventory of the area, we undertook the analysis of the surroundings – the functions of adjacent areas. Attention was drawn to the proximity of a school and kindergarten as well as playgrounds and sports fields. We analyzed communication needs taking into account existing desire lines and the frequency of their use.


## Square site development project

On the eastern side, beyond the boundary of the study, directly adjacent to the residential building, there is an area developed with numerous shrub and tree plantings, spatially enclosed by a formed hedge. From the southern and western sides, granite cube pavements adjoin the lawn. This is a remnant of the pre-war street grid. The roadside verges serve as parking for residents of nearby buildings. From the side of the kindergarten fence, there is a strip of high greenery.

Conclusions from the pre-design analyses provided the basis for the project – it was assumed that the Psia Górka pocket park project aims to create a place for passive recreation. The proposed directions of site development, including among others the type of terrain relief shaping and the character and type of greenery, the number and type of seats, were consulted with a residents' representative – the leader of the implemented WBO (Wrocław Participatory Budget) project.


## Design solutions

A diversification of the area will be five mounds, partly planted with shrubs, which will facilitate their maintenance, and covered with grass. Through one of them, a passage-tunnel made of a concrete prefabricate secured with a layer of rubber granulate in turquoise color was led. It leads to a small square strewn with bark, where there are seats and a tree branch. The central point of the small park is intended to serve as a meeting place for dog owners.

Entrances to the study area were accentuated by means of surfaces – round concrete slabs. The circle motif also appears in the seats, which are placed in several locations. All furniture is painted in blue and orange. Thanks to this, they constitute a distinctive element that will be associated with the park. Most importantly, many new trees and shrubs with high color variability depending on the season will appear in the park area. Six trees will be planted, such as sweetgum or ginkgo. In the selection of shrubs, it was decided to use species such as winged spindle or ninebark. The introduction of medium and high greenery will certainly positively affect the increase of the biocenotic value of the area and the improvement of local climate conditions.


## Effects and agreements

All design works were continuously agreed with the Zarząd Zieleni Miejskiej in Wrocław and the Project Leader. As part of the contract, the necessary permits of the Wydział Nieruchomości Komunalnych of the Urząd Miejski Wrocławia were also obtained.
