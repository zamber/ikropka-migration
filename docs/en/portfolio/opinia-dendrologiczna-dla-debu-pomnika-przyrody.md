## Dendrology opinion

Work on the opinion was preceded by a detailed tree inventory in the surroundings of the building planned for reconstruction and extension. The inventory identified 19 specimens of trees and shrubs. On the adjacent plot grows a monumental pedunculate oak with a circumference of 451 cm.

Due to the monument protection of the said oak, the Department of Environment and Agriculture of the Wrocław Municipal Office recommended the preparation of a dendrology study assessing the impact of the planned investment on the condition of the tree.

The assessment of the impact of the planned investment on the tree consisted of: assessment of the sanitary condition of the tree, analysis of geotechnical and site conditions in its surroundings, and a detailed analysis of the project – planned construction and execution solutions.


## Recommendations and monitoring

In the field, detailed dendrological inspections were carried out, basic dendrometric measurements were taken, and in-depth tree diagnostics were performed using, among others, an arborist probe, a rubber mallet, and other tools. The inspections showed that the root system, trunk, and crown of the tree are in good condition.

Subsequently, the design of the planned investment was analyzed in terms of its impact on the said monumental tree. Conclusions from the analysis and recommendations aimed at protecting the tree were formulated. The focus was mainly on the protection of the root system in relation to the geotechnical opinion specifying soil and water conditions.

The developed recommendations and guidelines will allow reconciling the implementation of the investment with the protection of the tree and its habitat. To achieve this goal, a necessary condition is ensuring dendrological supervision during the implementation of the investment, both at the planning stage and strictly construction works. Tree monitoring should be continued also after the completion of the construction investment for a period of minimum 3 years.
