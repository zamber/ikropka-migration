Plac Akademicki in Bytom is located in the central part of the city, near the Market Square. It can be considered a route connecting the Market Square with the cultural zone of the city – the Śląski Teatr Tańca and a jazz club. The location played a significant role in the concept of site development of the place.

The study area is specific in that it serves both as a residential square used by local residents and as a space utilized by the general public of the city. It was precisely these features that we decided to use and combine in the proposed concept.

Designers: A. Dobosz, E. Gillner, D. Krop-Andrzejczuk.


## Project description

The layout of the whole is based on a central square in the shape of a snail shell, with surrounding and radially departing flower beds and a symmetrical framework of the main pedestrian circulation routes. The central point of the square is an existing fountain in a slightly altered form. A "dancing path" runs through the area, consolidating the cultural zones of the city located diagonally across the square.

The entire concept emphasizes the cultural traditions of the city. The winding path leads us through several stops with information boards. The central layout elements are a stage and a playground. The stage together with the audience area is situated on a raised part of the terrain. Summer concerts of the nearby Dance Theatre and jazz club can be held there. The playground also fits into the shape of a snail shell. Through the forms of play equipment it refers to music. The "sound playground" may be equipped with a piano-shaped sandpit with key-seats, or other sound devices or toys (xylophones, humming pipes, telegraph).

Elements of the site worthy of development are the existing shelters, which in the concept were treated as an additional attraction of the place. The first of them can be used for the needs of the nearby stage, the second for organizing a mini art gallery in it. At the main entrance to the study area, in the location of the existing café, an extension of the gastronomic and service zone was proposed.

Also at night the square can become an attractive place. Lighting along the main pedestrian path accentuates not only the circulation route, but also the symmetrical main axis directed towards the clinic building. Additional light effects accentuate the central square and the stage.

The plant layout around the central square is radial. In this part of the square, ornamental perennial species are proposed to be introduced. The shape of these beds is meant to be associated with piano keys. Further on, the square is surrounded by arcuately arranged flower beds. Easily accessible passages to the lawns were left, which can be used for passive recreation. Beyond the "snail layout" with its imposed, geometric arrangement of beds, there are free plantings of existing trees. Supplementation of the avenue along the streets surrounding the square was also proposed.
