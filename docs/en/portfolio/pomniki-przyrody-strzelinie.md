Three old "Cross Oaks" established as a group natural monument were subjected to an expert assessment (authored by PAK IKROPKA), and subsequently to the tree care treatments resulting therefrom.


## Project Description

Due to the presence of hermit beetle *Osmoderma eremita* habitats on the trees, the scope and course of the tree care treatments were agreed with the Regional Directorate for Environmental Protection. By decision of the RDOŚ, the tree care treatments were carried out under dendrological and entomological supervision. IKROPKA, represented by Dominika Krop – Andrzejczuk, provided dendrological supervision.

Field inspection confirmed the colonization of all three monument oaks by the strictly protected beetle species, the hermit beetle. As part of the care, using rope techniques, a qualified arborist performed the removal of deadwood and the installation of four elastic bracing systems on one of the monument oaks. The felled branches were free of hermit beetle presence; therefore, no additional measures, e.g. translocation of developmental stages, were undertaken.

On one of the oaks, due to extensive internal chimney decay, a load test was conducted using rope tensioning, which revealed a lack of full tree stability. In view of the above, it was recommended to monitor the tree in question.
