The area of the designed pocket park is a greenery strip in the shape of an elongated rectangle, separated from the space by adjacent roadways. The central part of the study area, leaning against the southern and eastern boundary, is occupied by a greenery belt with a double alternating tree avenue. Young, last year's linden plantings predominate. On the northern side, a commercial pavilion is located, which was decided to be screened with effectively flowering shrub plantings such as bladder senna or butterfly bush.

The pre-war composition of the square is completely illegible, having been erased by post-war site development: two diagonally running paths, one of which is currently unused, while benches and waste bins were recently placed along the other. Studies and analyses of maps and photographic materials from the interwar period allowed for the reading of the former composition of the square, which was based on a centrally arranged small square, placed on the axis of the entrance to the tenement house at ul. I. L. Pereca 24.

## Site development project of the square in the historic layout

The designed site development aims to create a place for passive recreation: a centrally located square with benches, whose form freely references the historic layout. Due to the new arrangement of the greenery strip, transplanting of four young trees is necessary.

The square space was created to facilitate communication – to a large extent it coincides with former desire lines. The metal seats were painted with white paint so that they would not overheat excessively during warm periods. The green layout will be diversified by beds with shrubs and perennials in shades of pink and purple, whose free form references the development of the playground adjacent to the pocket park.

All design work was continuously coordinated with the Zarząd Zieleni Miejskiej in Wrocław and the Project Leader. As part of the contract, the necessary permits from ZDiUM, WUOZ and MKZ in Wrocław were also obtained.
