Urban biodiversity on a micro scale – a kindergarten ecosystem as a refuge of nature and a place for free play.


## Design Concept: learning through experiencing nature

**CONCEPT: LEARNING THROUGH EXPERIENCING NATURE**

**A garden that teaches and entertains.** The designed space is more than just a playground – it is a living nature laboratory, created for free play while simultaneously shaping pro-environmental attitudes in the youngest. The main objective of the project was the site development at a newly established kindergarten complex in Wrocław as a **jordan garden – educational garden**, where play naturally intertwines with ecological education. The project was developed in variants enabling applications for grants for pro-environmental activities.

**Nature education zones**

The kindergarten garden was divided into functional zones, each fulfilling a specific educational goal:

- **Grove and Forest Base.** The green space design provides for planting a compact group of trees and shrubs, which retains rainfall water and creates a natural microclimate (cooling through water transpiration on hot days). This is a place for lessons on the ecosystem role of trees and a home for small mammals – including in the "no-leaf-raking" zones, **hedgehog houses** were hidden.
- **Little Gardener Zone.** The kindergarten garden project includes, as part of the functional program, equipment with raised boxes (tiered vegetable beds) and a composter. Children can independently grow herbs and vegetables here, learning the life cycle of plants and the process of creating natural fertilizer (compost) from organic waste.
- **World of Insects and Amphibians.** The green space design includes the introduction of greenery with retention functions, but also attractive to small children, encouraging them to encounter and learn about nature – colorful perennial beds and flower meadows are a dining room for pollinators. Complementing these are **insect houses** and **boulders**, on which e.g. lizards can bask, as well as where smaller organisms can function. This is a space for observing biodiversity and learning respect for the smallest inhabitants of the garden.
- **Natural play and microclimate.** The project abandoned artificial materials – equipment and surfaces – in favor of natural and permeable materials – bark and sand. Such a solution allows the soil to "breathe", supports retention of rainfall water and prevents the formation of "heat islands". Play elements also have an educational character, enabling outdoor educational activities, e.g.: **entomologist play**. Bug-shaped spring riders and a spatial spider web are a pretext for movement play combined with learning about the world of invertebrates.
- **Green walls.** The building elevations were greened with climbers, creating vertical gardens that provide shelter for birds, supporting biodiversity, insulate the building, acting like free air conditioning. Climbers cool the external surroundings through water transpiration, lowering the temperature (similarly to trees) by a few degrees.


## Effects and implementation

The entire layout is a coherent ecosystem in which children become researchers and caretakers of nature, and the garden genuinely supports urban biodiversity.

The project was created for the needs of a grant application. An integral part alongside the graphic and drawing part was the descriptive part with a project description, activities fitting into the ideas of the grant project, as well as argumentation regarding meeting the grant criteria.
