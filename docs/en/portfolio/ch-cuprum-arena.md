A modern-style commercial gallery building based on a circular plan with entrances from three sides defined the character and layout of the site development around it.


## Project Description

As part of the study, a site development project, a construction project for greenery, a concept design of a "jumping fountain", a concept design of landscape furniture elements, a design for the selection of catalogue landscape furniture elements, a design of pedestrian-vehicular routes and pavement layout, and a lighting design with selection of lighting elements were prepared.

The green space design at Cuprum Arena takes into account the guidelines regarding permissible distances for planting trees and shrubs from buildings and infrastructure devices. All plants were selected appropriately to the future growing sites, with particular consideration of sunlight exposure and groundwater levels.

The space for vegetation in the area of entrance A is strongly limited due to the anticipated high intensity of pedestrian-vehicular traffic (pedestrian crossings and entrance to the rotunda with a "snail"). In this area, only low vegetation in the form of shrub avenues was therefore used, emphasizing the pedestrian routes. Perennial flower beds and ground-covering vegetation in contrasting compositions provide complement. Along the outline of the rotunda, from the southern and eastern sides, climbing plants will be planted in a specially designed planter. Led on steel cables, they will create a green elevation of this part of the commercial facility.

The area of entrance B is a zone with the most representative character, which after the opening of the commercial gallery is to become the new centre of Lubin. When planning this zone, the building mass with the huge glazed entrance area was taken into account, but also the anticipated high intensity of pedestrian traffic. A wide alley adapted for vehicular traffic (greenery service, technical services, emptying of waste bins, etc.) leads to the main entrance. Access to this zone from ul. gen. W. Sikorskiego is additionally provided by two paths. The central point is a water element in the form of a fountain located in the middle of the square. In this area, an amphitheatre incorporated into the slope from the side of ul. gen. W. Sikorskiego was planned. On the other side of the stage, on the wall surrounding the square, additional seating for several dozen people was provided. From the side of the Baczyna channel, a gently sloping embankment was planned to be raised. In this place, medium and tall vegetation was decided to be used, which will allow hiding from the eyes of gallery customers the unattractive neighbourhood on the other bank of the Baczyna channel.

When planning the spatial layout of the area of entrance C, the close proximity of the market square and the location of public transport stops were primarily taken into account. The layout of paths also includes easy pedestrian access to the footbridges over the Baczyna channel, and consequently to entrance C itself, located on the other bank of the channel. The tall vegetation of this area consists of the existing lime tree avenue, which is proposed to be supplemented with missing specimens.
