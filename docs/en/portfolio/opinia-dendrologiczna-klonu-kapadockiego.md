The prepared dendrology opinion aimed to determine the health condition of the tree and to issue recommendations regarding proper protection of the tree during the planned park revitalization investment.


## Project Description

The opinion was conducted on the basis of the tree inventory PAK IKROPKA from May 2018, as well as historical research of the Youth Park by dr I. Bińkowska.

During field works, detailed dendrological inspections and measurements were carried out, and photographic documentation was prepared.


## Challenges

Root system probing was performed using a root probe, and the presence of any defects in the lower part of the trunk was checked using a rubber mallet. The sanitary condition of the tree and the scope of necessary care treatments were assessed. Great importance was attached to the verification of the tree's surroundings. The tree grows in high density, in the central part of the park, and currently no path is located within the range of its crown. The circumference of the tree in question is 360 cm.


## Solutions

The health condition of the tree was determined as good. Care treatments using climbing techniques were recommended, establishing requirements for the contractor – a certified arborist with 3 years of experience working with monumental trees or trees growing in historic areas.

Numerous recommendations regarding the protection of the tree during the planned investment (park revitalization) were issued. Thanks to the professionally prepared study, there is a real chance to preserve this extraordinary tree in good condition. In accordance with the recommendations already at the stage of planning the construction site organization, the tree in question will be covered by special protection. A Tree Protection Zone was also designated for the tree, within which works are prohibited. It was also recommended to carry out works under the supervision of a green space inspector with experience in dendrology.


## Results

Cappadocian maple (Acer cappadocicum) is an extremely valuable species in ornamental plantings, collectible. In Lower Silesia, as well as in the whole of Poland, there are very few specimens of this species. The Cappadocian maple from the Youth Park is in good condition, has a beautiful habit, as well as a considerable circumference (360 cm). Due to the fact that the tree in question has enormous natural, landscape and scientific value, and stands out against other trees occurring in the park and in the whole city, it was suggested to designate it as a natural monument. A tree of this species with such parameters constitutes a true dendrological curiosity. The maple in question may become a natural and tourist attraction.
