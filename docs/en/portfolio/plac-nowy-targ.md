Plac Nowy Targ has served market functions for centuries, although unlike the Rynek or Plac Solny, it has never been a particularly representative place. Currently, a significant part of the square serves as a car park, being neither a resident-friendly space nor useful or functional. The buildings around the square mainly serve residential and administrative functions.


## Project Description

The competition design concept is an attempt to restore the former glory of this area, to create a new public space full of life, recognizable by tourists and residents. A combination of symmetry and diversity was chosen, operating with contrast in terms of form and materials. The inspiration for the project was a pane of broken glass, which at the same time is a symbolic reference to the wartime destruction during Festung Breslau, and also serves as a starting point for the idea of creating very dynamic pavement forms.


## Design Solutions

The designed main visual axis of the square runs in accordance with urban planning guidelines. The design restores the market function of the square through designed demountable market stalls of light steel structure, the construction of a commercial and service pavilion, the so-called Miejski Pavilion, and the creation of an attractive center of the square with a fountain. Additionally, the design locates functional landscape furniture and lighting (street lighting and luminaire fixtures mounted in the square pavement, meeting the requirements of separate regulations regarding protection against glare). The concept adopts the exclusion of vehicular traffic from the square area, enabling free pedestrian movement and the establishment of an underground car park.

In the northern part of the square, high greenery was designed in the form of three avenues of ginkgo biloba cv. 'Saratoga' planted parallel to the northern frontage of the square. Low greenery was designed in the form of raised planters above the floor level. The material, form and color scheme of the planters create a coherent whole and refer to the pattern of the square pavement, and their edges may serve as additional seating. In the planters, plantings of carex pendula were designed – a dense turf of intense green color. An automatic irrigation system was designed for all greenery elements.

In order to ensure optimal living conditions for the designed trees, a root ball aeration system was applied, located in basins 2 m deep. The upper surfaces of the flower beds in which the trees were placed were covered with a grate protecting against trampling.

Materials used in the concept: steel structures, stone cladding slabs, self-cleaning tempered glass in titanium color, polished granite slabs, gres slabs, corten steel sheet slabs.
