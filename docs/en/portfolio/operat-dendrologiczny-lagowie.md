In Łagów near Zgorzelec, we carried out a tree inventory with tree stand management. The subject area is the site of a former manor farm located within the village of Łagów in a linear village layout, where a palace (currently restored) was situated with an adjacent park and pond.

The tree stand inventoried within the study area consists partly of large-area groups of shrubs and self-sown seedlings overgrowing the neglected greenery area, but also dendrological curiosities of considerable girth.

## Project Description

During field works, each of the trees and shrubs was mapped, inventoried, described, and subjected to dendrometric measurements along with a sanitary condition assessment. The management plan was prepared from the sanitary and care perspective. In the case of trees in poor condition, posing a threat to the safety of people and property, sanitary removal was recommended. For the remaining trees, necessary care treatments were recommended, such as the removal of deadwood or the installation of bracing and adaptations.

The oldest trees within the study area are pedunculate oaks, wych elms, and silver maples reaching girths of up to 470 cm, as well as ornamental varieties of common beech ‘Atropurpurea’ and pedunculate oak ‘Fastigiata’.

## Outcomes

Thanks to the prepared study, the Client gained precise knowledge of the existing greenery, as well as its health status and the scope of required care. The study was prepared in accordance with applicable regulations.
