The project was developed as part of the task entitled Expansion of the educational centre for hearing-impaired children at ul. Dworska in Wrocław.


## Project description

The study area is a manor park. The area can be divided into a regular part with avenue plantings and ornamental plant species, forming the axis between the entrance to the site and the building of the former manor house, and an irregular part situated in the northern part of the site – with old-growth trees and "wild" shrubbery. Among the old-growth trees, pedunculate oaks and hedgerows of common beeches stand out, as well as lindens, mainly Crimean lindens.

As part of the study, historical and spatial analyses of the park layout were carried out, age analyses of the park tree stand and a tree inventory with a tree stand management design, followed by a concept design, construction project and building project concluded with technical specifications of works, measurements of works and the investor's cost estimate.

The project does not change the function or purpose of the plot, but includes the valorization of the park together with landscape furniture, designs for the reconstruction of existing roads and paths and the construction of new ones, designs for the renovation of the existing playground, clearing and rubble removal, demolition of remnants of existing foundations of non-historic buildings, and dismantling of unnecessary internal fences.

On the plot, paths were designed in a layout referencing the historical one, through which a nature-educational trail with information boards was led. This trail, with a length of approx. 1.5 km, comprises 13 stops that are attractive from a natural perspective – interesting tree and animal species as well as attractive views were included there.

The designed vegetation cover serves a compensatory function – in connection with the necessary tree and shrub removals for the expansion of the educational centre. The green space design takes local conditions into account – the park area is located partly on the flood polders of the Ślęza river.


## Implementation

The project was completed under author supervision.
