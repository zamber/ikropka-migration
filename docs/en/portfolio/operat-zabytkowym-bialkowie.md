The subject of the study was a tree inventory together with a tree management plan from a sanitary perspective. The area covered by the study is a historic palace park entered in the register of monuments and the plot area where a contemporary astronomical observatory is located.

The park is currently neglected, heavily overgrown with self-seeded vegetation, and the historic path communication layout has not been preserved either. It requires tidying up and carrying out care works on part of the tree stand.


## Project Description

The oldest trees within the study area are oaks reaching girths of about 380 cm and a historic linden with a girth above 370 cm. The study included inventory of the tree stand, assessment of its sanitary condition and determination of the necessary care treatments and sanitary removals (due to poor sanitary condition).

Out of 503 inventoried trees of the tree stand, 59 trees and shrubs were designated for care. Recommended care treatments include, among others, removal of deadwood, control and installation of elastic cobra-type bindings, crown shaping.

In the descriptive part of the study, a division was made into trees located within the range of the monument and outside it. The study was prepared in accordance with applicable regulations.
