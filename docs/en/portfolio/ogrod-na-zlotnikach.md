The garden design concept combines geometric and free-flowing layouts in the minimalist style of an urban garden.


## Project Description

From the entrance to the house door leads a winding path, and the building mass has been emphasized by arc-shaped curved beds, on which shrubs have been planted exposed with gravel mulch. In the front part, two magnificent trees were located together with shade-loving perennial plantings at the ground level, flanked by ornamental shrubs. On the other side of the garden, additional parking spaces were placed, made of lawn grid allowing for discreet blending into the garden layout.

Rectangular planters add character to the space. The recreational part of the garden is divided into two sections by a hedge of catalpas, ornamental shrubs, and perennials flowering at different times of the year. The planting strip closes off the terrace from the garden part with a large lawn. This optically divided the spacious garden interior, making the atmosphere more intimate. Around the plot, the fencing was enhanced – ornamental shrubs and a regular composition of dwarf willows are visible from the house windows, but also constitute an aesthetic screen from the utility zone and the street.
