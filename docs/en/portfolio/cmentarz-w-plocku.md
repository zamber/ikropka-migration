Płock, Masovian Voivodeship. Competition for the development of public space.

The Evangelical-Augsburg cemetery in Płock was established at the beginning of the 19th century. The necropolis is divided into two parts: the northern sacral part with the parish chapel and utility buildings, and the southern burial part – a heavily wooded fragment.


## Project description

The study prepared monument protection guidelines regarding the introduction of new plantings and the location of new burials, as well as a site development project containing the programme for activating the cemetery together with the green space design. The green space design was preceded by an inventory and a management plan for the existing tree stand.


## Design solutions

Trees and shrubs in poor health condition and up to 10 years of age were designated for removal. Compositional removals were carried out. This aimed to organize the cemetery space, restore visual connections with the surroundings, create new burial places, and provide light to the ground before introducing low ornamental vegetation. The species selection of new plantings was inspired by natural plant communities, particularly oak-hornbeam forest communities.

Tree avenues were introduced into the cemetery space, thereby emphasizing the layout of the designed avenues. The avenues growing in the outer parts were supplemented with new plantings.
