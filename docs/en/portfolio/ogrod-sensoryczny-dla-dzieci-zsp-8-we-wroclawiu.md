Prior to commencing design works, a comprehensive tree inventory was carried out together with a tree stand management plan. In the prepared study, an assessment of tree safety was performed, and care recommendations were issued.


## Project Description

In the developed design concept, we made every effort to ensure that the sensory garden provides its visitors with numerous sensory experiences, stimulating fascination and curiosity about the natural world. We believe that learning through play combined with children's interaction with nature has a significant impact on well-being, and is also an excellent way to stimulate imagination and creativity.


## Challenges

The priority and challenge undertaken in the design of the sensory playground was the combination of therapeutic and educational functions. This goal was achieved through carefully considered landscape furniture elements, as well as through the selection of vegetation and materials acting upon each of the senses.


## Solutions

The concept of the unconventional playground consists in creating separate, harmonized zones, which positively affects perception, the sense of safety and user comfort. Contact with nature, even through its passive observation, significantly reduces the level of stress and anxiety. The created open-air classrooms allow for conducting interesting and non-standard lessons in the soothing shade of trees and surrounding vegetation.

In the selection of greenery, we were guided by safety as well as the possibility of creating an interesting colour and fragrance composition. We made every effort so that the play zone delights with its beauty in different seasons, where the opportunity to observe the occurring changes is an interesting experience for young naturalists.

The proposed vegetable gardens are an excellent example where play intertwines with learning and the active participation of children, and its effects are very tasty.

The combination of natural materials such as wood or stone further strongly affects the perception of nature, allowing one to notice its charm and richness of forms, colours and texture. Therefore, in the site development project at ZSP 8, all landscape furniture elements proposed by us contain these features, where their use is a true pleasure and joy.
