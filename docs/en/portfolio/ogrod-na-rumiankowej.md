As part of the order, a tree inventory and a design concept for a home garden were carried out.


## Project Description

In the design concept, several functional zones were designated. The circulation zone is located on the eastern and western side of the building. It connects the entrance area with the terrace and provides access from the garage, through the utility zone, to the terrace. The utility zone is located in the eastern part of the plot.

One tree was designated for removal due to excessive planting density. The remaining existing trees were preserved.

Children's play zones occupy a large part of the garden, as they will be its main users. The passive play zone for children is located south of the terrace. It is separated by a different type of surface and small shrubs, safe and child-friendly. On the southern side, this zone is closed off by an embankment in which a tunnel is placed. The active play zone for children is located in the southern part of the garden. It includes the existing sledding hill, and it is planned to be separated from the rest of the garden by two gentle, grassy mounds. Inside the space created by the elevations, a trampoline is placed.

Between the passive and active zones, it is proposed to place attractive play elements, e.g. a willow tunnel or a barefoot path.

The relaxation zone is located in the eastern part of the garden, intended mainly for adult users. It is a shaded place where one can relax on a sun lounger or hammock.

The place for a vegetable garden is located in the western part of the garden. The entrance is from the southern side, so that from the direction of the house entrance only the mound is visible. In the eastern slope of the cellar, a recess with a countertop for meal preparation is incorporated. Next to it is a place for a grill functionally connected with the terrace. Adjacent to the terrace is a bed of culinary herbs.

By the terrace, planting of climbers on a pergola is planned. The purpose of this measure is to shield the terrace from the view of future neighbors and to enrich the aesthetic-visual values of this part of the garden. In the north-western part of the plot, by the fence, a screening bed protecting the interior of the garden is planned. To the existing plantings, new species of shrubs and perennials were added in a naturalistic style.
