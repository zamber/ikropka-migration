The design task consisted of creating two different design concepts for the re-arrangement of the grounds adjacent to the factory buildings. The main objective of the Client was to redesign these spaces with the Employees in mind – to create a relaxation space – a pleasant place to spend work breaks. Furthermore, a representative entrance zone was conceived.

## Project Description

The concepts were preceded by a site visit, during which a site and greenery inventory was carried out. Following preliminary design analyses, a programmatic-spatial concept was developed, which allowed for the organization of the area into main zones: two relaxation zones, a smoking zone, and an entrance zone.

Concept authors: D. Krop-Andrzejczuk, K. Mazur, J. Trzaska.

## Two Arrangement Concepts

Two concepts for the new arrangement of the company's space were developed.

The first, based on the idea of upcycling, utilizes materials derived from the secondary use of waste. Elements such as wooden EUR-pallets and industrial steel barrels were applied. The colour scheme is dominated by white and steel complemented by blue referencing the company logotype. Greenery was planned in the form of low trees or tall multi-stemmed shrubs accompanied by ornamental grasses and succulent perennials.

The second concept introduces an industrial style – simple, linear forms and uniform colours of classic black metal elements and natural wood brown as well as muted concrete grey. The whole is complemented by light, elegant greenery based on various varieties of grasses, perennials and climbers.
