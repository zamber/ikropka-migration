The monumental Cross Oak under dendrological supervision by IKROPKA.


## Project Description

The monumental Cross Oak is one of three trees that together form the group natural monument "Dęby Krzyżowe". Due to its condition, Gmina Strzelin commissioned IKROPKA to prepare a specialist dendrology expert assessment in the context of sanitary condition and the scope of care and maintenance works with regard to safety.

As part of the assessment, we performed dendrometric measurements of the tree, thoroughly examined its sanitary condition by analysing the preservation state of the trunk and crown, as well as the site and soil conditions within the root system. We compared the tree's condition with archival data from 2015, when we prepared a joint study – a dendrology expert assessment of all three Cross Oaks.

The subject tree – a pedunculate oak (*Quercus robus*) with a circumference of 478 cm, height of 10 m, crown diameter of 5×4 m – turned out to be in poor sanitary condition. At the same time, the habitat of a protected beetle species – the hermit beetle (*Osmoderma eremita*) – was confirmed.


## Solution

The recommendation of the assessment was to install three steel braces on the tree trunk at different heights. This action is intended to ensure safety in the immediate vicinity of the tree, as well as to protect the habitat of the protected hermit beetle.

The recommended care works were agreed upon, among others, with the Regional Directorate for Environmental Protection in Wrocław.

The execution of the securing works proceeded under dendrological supervision provided by IKROPKA Inspectors: Dominika Krop-Andrzejczuk and Paweł Andrzejczuk, entomological supervision was provided by IKROPKA's permanent collaborator dr Marcin Kadej, and the contractor of the arboricultural works themselves was Dendrobates Wojciech Trzonkowski.


## Outcomes

Thanks to the exemplary conduct of the actions, it was possible to secure both the surroundings of the tree – the proximity of pedestrian and cycling tourist trails, the natural monument itself, as well as the habitat located inside the trunk.
