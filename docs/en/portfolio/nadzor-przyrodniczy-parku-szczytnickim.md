Recently, we provided nature supervision – dendrological and entomological – over the tree removal (silver linden) growing in Park Szczytnicki in Wrocław. The tree removal was necessary for safety reasons. In this case, the permit for removal was issued by the Municipal Monument Conservator (the area is entered in the monument registers). Due to the fact that the tree was inhabited by protected insect species: the hermit beetle and the great capricorn beetle, an appropriate decision of the Regional Directorate for Environmental Protection in Wrocław was also obtained for the destruction of the habitat of protected species and the relevant exemptions from the prohibitions arising from the Nature Conservation Act. The conditions of the RDOŚ decision obligated the removal to be carried out under entomological supervision.


## Tree inhabited by protected species

The contractor of the works engaged our company to conduct supervisory activities. The works were carried out under the direction of the dendrology supervision inspector mgr inż. arch. kraj. Dominika Krop-Andrzejczuk in cooperation with the nature supervision inspector, entomologist dr inż. Marcin Kadej. The tree was removed by an experienced team of arborists – in sections. Each fragment of the woody debris was carefully checked for colonization by protected insects. The larvae of the hermit beetle and the great capricorn beetle were extracted and transferred to a place enabling their further development. The decaying wood habitat was properly secured and transported together with a part of the tree to a location agreed with the site manager – a Natura 2000 area, enabling the completion of the development of the developmental forms of protected insect species. We trust that the mission will end in full success, giving nature in the near future another generation of adult forms capable of reproduction. A complicated but very satisfying operation.


## Summary

We invite all investors, managers, and contractors to cooperate in similar nature supervisions. The applied minimization of loss in relation to the species is always better than renouncing this type of rescue actions. However, it should be remembered that this is a last resort and should be applied only in justified cases. For it is always better to protect than to eliminate.
