The study area is located in the small village of Uskorz Mały, near Wołów, in a setting surrounded by forests, wild nature, meadows and numerous ponds. The green space design was composed in reference to the character of the surroundings, opting for free compositions of gentle meanders and arcs.

The layout of vegetation appears to be a continuation of the beautiful, naturalistic vicinity. Due to the large area of the plot, several garden interiors were designated, separated by extensive beds of flowering perennials, shrubs and ornamental grasses.

## Project description

As part of the project, the following were carried out in stages: tree inventory with a forest stand management design, concepts for greenery site development together with landscape furniture proposals, and finally – green space design together with landscape furniture and lighting design in terms of location. The design work proceeded in cooperation and consultation with the architects responsible for the building design and the designed hard surfaces on the site.

One of the greatest assets of the designed garden is the existing pond with an island. Several intimate garden interiors will be created in the garden, including on the island, a relaxation spot by the pier, a nook with a swing by the house, and a viewpoint with a bench on the peninsula of the pond. The area on the other side of the house is a more natural character zone. The eastern part, being better lit, was designated for a flowering meadow surrounded by wild thickets, with a viewpoint near the house. The western part was planned as a typically forested section, with shade-loving vegetation, where a wild thicket with wood anemone stands was preserved. In the entrance zone, two large groups of heath plants, rhododendrons and azaleas were designed.

The green space design included plant compositions, guidelines for the proper execution of beds and plantings, a specification of the designed plant material with quantities, mulching material under vegetation, instructions for establishing lawns, beds and flowering meadows, as well as a care calendar – maintenance recommendations for the designed vegetation for each month.
