The subject of the study is the revitalization of a small square at the intersection of ul. Afgańska and ul. Pakistańska in Brochów, Wrocław. The square is located in the central part of the housing estate, bordered on each side by multifamily buildings with services on the ground floor. The users of the space are local residents and customers of service premises. The main design assumption was to create a green space supporting the neighborhood integration of Brochów residents, and the project itself was developed in consultation and co-financed by the Brochów District Council.

## Project description

The site development project assumes the retention of the existing communication layout, organizing the square's space and widening the communication route on the northern side. In the widened communication strip, integrative tables with seating were designed, also adapted to the needs of persons with reduced mobility, along with benches, low greenery, and two-species avenue tree plantings. The entire zone is crowned with atmospheric lighting in the form of a garland, thereby creating an intimate, cozy integrative space. The central part of the park is a recreational glade – a space left at the disposal of residents, dedicated to picnicking and outdoor sports activities.

## Site development details

In the southern part of the layout is a monument dedicated to the memory of fallen railway workers, for which a design for relocation to the other side of the communication route was developed, thereby improving the functional and spatial layout of the designed space.

Additional elements of the development will include a drinking fountain providing access to potable water, bicycle racks, litter bins, benches and seating on a raised wall on the northern side, and information boards.

The greenery layout is based on avenue plantings from the street side using two alternately planted species: Dutch lime 'Euchlora' (Tilia ×europaea 'Euchlora') and Turkish hazel (Corylus colurna). In total, 44 avenue trees are designed. Both species tolerate urban conditions well, have dense foliage and a similar habit. Planting more than one species is one of the strategies preparing urban greenery for climate change. Greater biological diversity also ensures food for pollinators over a longer period of the year. The niche of the relocated monument will be flanked by two London plane trees (Platanus ×hispanica). The high greenery will be supplemented by shrub and perennial beds.
