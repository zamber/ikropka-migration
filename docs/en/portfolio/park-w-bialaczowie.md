The study concerns a historic palace and park complex. The scope of work included, among others, preparation of a historical query, tree inventory and tree stand management design, park revitalization plan, water and legal permit documentation, building and construction project, coordinated with the monument conservator. As a result, a water and legal permit and a building permit were obtained.


## Project Description

Most of the park tree stand was destroyed in 2011 by a storm, therefore when commencing the project, difficulties were encountered in reading the historic layout of the composition. The avenue leading from the palace building towards the town hall was completely devastated, and also few trees survived at the palace driveway and by the farm buildings. The state of preservation of the historic park meant that the works carried out were of a reconstructive nature. The idea of the project was to adapt the designed greenery to the preserved and non-preserved park dendroflora, to the specificity of the site, local conditions and the vicinity of other plants. The project was executed with care for visual and aesthetic values.


## Solutions

In terms of species selection, the prepared tree inventory was used as a basis, as well as analyses and a historical query. The performed dendrochronological analysis allowed determination of the age of the existing and former tree stand. Wherever possible, in places where historic tree stand had grown, the same species were planted. Several tree species not recorded on the park premises were also introduced; however, they correspond with the decorative character of the park and the tendencies to plant acclimatized species, present in garden art since the mid-18th century. An orchard was designed in the north-eastern part. Thanks to preserved records concerning the orchard at the Białaczów folwark, it is known which varieties of apple, pear and plum trees were planted there. The designed historic varieties correspond to those once cultivated in Białaczów.


## Effects

Ornamental shrubs were planted near the buildings, and rose gardens were laid out in front of the palace, on the southern side. Behind the palace there is an ornamental bed in the form of a garden parterre.
