The study was prepared to assess the impact of the conducted crown pruning on the condition of a tree of the species common rowan. The expert assessment was also intended to determine whether the performed works complied with the applicable Nature Conservation Act and the scope indicated in the decision of the Municipal Monument Conservator in Płock.


## Description of the expert assessment

During field works, detailed dendrological inspections, measurements, and an assessment of the sanitary condition of the tree were carried out. Particular attention was paid to the wound resulting from recently performed care treatments, as well as the condition of the root system from the northern side, where the cut was made, was verified. This location was exposed to root decay; however, examination with an arboricultural probe did not reveal rot.


## Results and conclusions

The sanitary condition of the rowan was determined as good. The tree is vital, showing no signs of reduced mechanical strength. As a result of removing a structural branch, a significant crown reduction was performed. An accurate assessment of the impact of the cut on the tree's health condition will be possible by observing the specimen over subsequent vegetation seasons – the inspections took place approximately one and a half months after the treatment, which is too short a period for such an assessment. It should be emphasized, however, that the tree is relatively young and healthy, and this species exhibits a relatively high ability to heal wounds.

The cuts were performed after obtaining the decision of the Municipal Conservator in Płock, which specified the permissible amount of removed branches in relation to the entire assimilative mass of the tree (15%), and also did not permit drastic cuts of branches exceeding 10 cm in circumference.

To assess the performed crown reduction, an analysis of archival photographs was conducted, comparing them with photographs showing the same view after the cuts. An archival satellite image and the crown spread measured in the field were also compared. The analyses allowed determining the percentage of crown mass loss.

The expert assessment showed that the performed treatment was carried out contrary to the decision of the Municipal Monument Conservator in Płock, because up to 26% of the crown mass was removed and a branch with a circumference of 58 cm was removed, which exceeds the recommended cut size by nearly six times. Nevertheless, under the Nature Conservation Act, the tree was neither damaged nor destroyed.
